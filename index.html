First step to building a skateboard is the board/deck. Deck size is a thing you will hear alot, board sizes vary from 7.5"-10" on average. There are bigger and smaller boards, but when picking out your first set up youll want to stay between 7.75"-8.5". Somewhere in the middle should be best, but it is a preference issue. Everyone has an opinion. The best way to pick a size is by standing on the board, feeling how wide and long it is and how the concave of the board feels under your feet. If it feels good to you, go with it. Of course the graphics do matter, if your skateboard looks cool you're going to want to skate it. Its totally normal to stand on the boards, dont be shy, ask whoever works there.

Trucks are a little easier to pick out, you'll pick the size of trucks that fit your board. Different truck companies have different ways of mesuring, 5.0's, 149's, 44's, its confusing at first but the shop employee should know the sizes. You'll understand it with time. Each truck brand feels a little different, its a preference issue again, but if you're new to skateboarding there might not be a preference. I suggest asking friends or even the shop employee what they would recommend, i like the feel of Indys or Aces.

wheels sizes are set up by millimeters and hardness by durometer. standard wheels sizes are between 50mm-56mm, somewhere in the middle should be fine. durometer is the hardness of your wheels, I do not recommend soft wheels if you want to learn tricks, soft wheels dont slide as great and usually weight more. bones and spitfires will probably be your best options. griptape is a preference like most things in skateboarding, theres nothing wrong with clear or colored grip but i dont suggest them for your first board. that being said, its your board, do what you'd like. hardwear is basic, just make sure you get long enough hardwear, I suggest inch long hardwear unless you deside to get risers.

Make sure you go to a skater owned and operated skateshop if you have that option. those shops do more for the skate community in your area, like contest and sponsorships, so its very important to shop there and support them so they can support you. also the mall shops or chain shops dont always have skateboarders working there, they have sales people, so you could end up getting a sets up that isnt best for you.
